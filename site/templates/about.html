How this works

An automated pipeline searches several news sources every two hours, scores candidate articles for relevance against a fixed definition for each of the five areas above, and writes a short original summary for everything that passes. We never reproduce source article text or images — only the headline (linked to the original), the source name, the publish time, and our own one-sentence summary are shown. Summaries are generated by an AI model and may occasionally be imprecise; always consult the original source for the full story.

On the China section

The China section applies the same factual relevance standard as every other section on this site. Items are selected because they are factually about China's involvement in AI fraud, pharma, Meta/platform activity, or data center infrastructure — not to support any particular narrative or conclusion.
